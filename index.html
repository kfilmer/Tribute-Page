Artemisia Gentileschi

The first woman to become a member of the Florence Academy of Fine Art

Key Points in Artemisia's Life:

  • Early Life
    • - Born in Rome, Italy on July 8, 1593
    • - Introduced to painting by her father after death of her mother in 1605
    • - Earliest suriving work is Susanna and the Elders, completed in 1610

  • Florentine Period (1612-1620)
    • - Became a successful court painter as patron of House of Medici
    • - Accepted into the Florence Academy of Fine Arts
    • - Completed Allegory of Inclination in 1611 for Casa Buonarroti, great-nephew of Michelangelo

  • Return to Rome (1620-1627)
    • - Reputation grew with influx of patrons

    • Venice (1627-1630)
      • - Moved to Venice in hopes of richer commissions

    • Naples and English Period (1630-1656)
      • - Relocated to Naples in 1630 in sear of new and more lucrative job opporunities
      • - Started working on painings in a cathedral for the first time
      • - The exact timing of her death is unknown, but it is believed to be in 1656 during a plague that swept Naples
